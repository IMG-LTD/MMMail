# MMMail Trademark Policy

This document sets a minimal public boundary for the MMMail name, marks, and product identity.

## Purpose

The goal is to let users describe compatibility and forks without creating confusion about official ownership, endorsement, or hosted service status.

## Allowed Use

You may use the MMMail name to:

- refer to the upstream open-source project
- describe that a fork is based on MMMail
- link to this repository
- document compatibility with MMMail APIs or deployment artifacts

Use should be factual and must make clear that no endorsement has been granted.

## Forks And Derivatives

Forks may say "based on MMMail" or "compatible with MMMail" when accurate.

Forks must not:

- do not present a fork as official MMMail
- use names that are confusingly similar to official MMMail release channels
- imply maintainer endorsement
- imply hosted service authorization
- imply commercial support authorization

## Hosted And Commercial Use

Do not advertise a hosted or managed service as official MMMail unless the maintainer has explicitly approved that use.

Commercial pilots, hosted operations, marketplace listings, and paid support pages must link to the current support and pricing boundaries:

- `docs/commercial/pricing-boundaries.md`
- `docs/commercial/support-policy.md`
- `docs/release/v2-support-boundaries.md`

## Attribution

When redistributing modified builds, preserve upstream license and attribution notices, including `LICENSE`, `NOTICE`, and frontend attribution where applicable.
